The Official Bylaws & Sacred Charter

of the Fermat Party Planning Committee, SF Chapter

Est. 2026 · 594 Howard St
§1
👑

Jurisdiction

The FPPC has sole dominion over all celebrations, morale-boosting events, and whether or not we spring for the good napkins. If you planned a birthday without us, it didn't happen.

Non-negotiable
§2
📌

Theme Proposals

All theme ideas are welcome. They will be discussed, debated, tabled, revisited, re-tabled, and ultimately replaced by something one of us saw on Pinterest 20 minutes before the event.

Creative process
§3
💸

The Budget

We have one. Don't ask how much. Just know that "tasteful" and "affordable" are two different things and we will be choosing a side.

Classified
§4
🚫

The Veto

Any committee member may veto a suggestion, but must provide an alternative that is equal to or more fun. Saying "we could just not do anything" is grounds for removal.

Fun is mandatory
§5
👀

Attendance

Attendance at FPPC-sanctioned events is technically optional but socially mandatory. We will notice if you're not there. We will remember. HR has nothing to do with this.

We see you
§6
⚔️

Rival Committees

There is no rival committee. If someone tries to start one, we do not acknowledge it. We are the only committee. There is only one committee. This bylaw exists as a formality.

There is no war
§7
🍻

Happy Hour Protocol

Happy hours shall occur no less than once per month. Venue selection is handled by the Wheel (see: Happy Hour tab). If the Wheel chooses poorly, blame physics — not the committee.

Sacred tradition
§8
💡

The Suggestion Box

Anyone may suggest an event via the dashboard. The FPPC will review all submissions with the seriousness they deserve. Which is to say: we'll read them during happy hour.

Democracy-ish

This document is binding, unappealable, and entirely made up.
Amendments require a 2/3 majority vote and at least one dessert bribe.

RATIFIED ✓
